A personal commitment

The Buy Ban

Now through June 28th

We came to acknowledge that our buying had become unmanageable — not because we lacked means, but because we had lost meaning. We scrolled, we saw, we purchased. We told ourselves it was a treat. But the high faded before the package arrived, and the cycle began again.

We recognized that fast fashion had made us its customer, not its beneficiary. That trends were designed to expire. That we had been buying an identity instead of building one.

And so, we choose to stop. Not out of deprivation — but out of intention.

The commitment

Until June 28th, we will not purchase clothing, jewelry, fragrances, or any item that is a want and not a need. We pause the cart. We close the tab. We let the impulse pass.

Eating out, gas, groceries, and necessary expenses are not restricted. This is not austerity — it is clarity.

When we feel the urge to buy, we ask ourselves: Do I need this, or do I just need to feel something? We sit with that question. We let it breathe.

We believe that a piece chosen slowly — researched, saved for, deliberated over — holds a weight that an impulse purchase never can. We want our closets and our collections to tell a story we actually chose to write. Not one the algorithm wrote for us.

I scroll, therefore I am is not an identity we wish to carry anymore.

What we are building toward

When June 28th arrives, we will not shop with urgency. We will shop with earned intention. Each piece will carry the story of what we did to acquire it — the patience, the restraint, the choice.

Sentimental value cannot be added to cart. It is built by time, by sacrifice, by knowing exactly why you chose a thing and what it cost you beyond money.

"We admitted that the scroll had become the purchase, and the purchase had become the void, and the void had become us — and we decided that was enough."
